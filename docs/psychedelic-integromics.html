Psychedelic Integromics Research

Understanding the Biological Signature of the Ayahuasca Experience

Psychedelics are experiencing a scientific renaissance. While clinical trials suggest therapeutic potential in depression, PTSD and addiction, the biological mechanisms underlying their effects in humans remain largely unknown.

This project investigates one central question:

What happens in the human metabolome during a psychedelic experience?

Our 2022 study, published in Biomedicine & Pharmacotherapy, represents the first report describing the impact of a psychedelic (ayahuasca) on the human metabolome.

Why Ayahuasca?

Ayahuasca is a traditional Amazonian brew prepared from:

  • Banisteriopsis caapi (Œ≤-carbolines: harmine, harmaline, tetrahydroharmine)
  • Psychotria viridis (DMT)

DMT activates 5-HT2A receptors, while Œ≤-carbolines inhibit monoamine oxidase (MAO), allowing DMT to become orally active.

Although subjective effects are well described, the downstream biochemical cascades triggered by 5-HT2A activation remain poorly understood

Study Design

We conducted a within-subject observational study in 23 healthy participants from the Santo Daime tradition.

Linking Biology to Subjective Experience

The integrated network analysis revealed:

  • Strong associations between LNAA balance and 9 of 11 ASC subscales
  • Positive association of tryptophan/LNAA ratio with experiences of:
    • Unity
    • Blissful state
    • Insightfulness

Remarkably, these metabolic signatures were only moderately related to plasma DMT concentrations.

This suggests that:

The subjective psychedelic experience may be shaped more by systemic metabolic reconfiguration than by drug concentration alone.

Systems-Level Interpretation

Enrichment analysis confirmed dysregulation in pathways involved in:

  • Serotonin synthesis
  • Dopamine metabolism
  • Endocannabinoid signalling

Together, the findings support a crosstalk between peripheral metabolism and central neurotransmission, revealing a specific metabolic fingerprint underlying the ayahuasca experience.

Scientific Rigor & Limitations

  • Naturalistic ceremonial setting
  • Single post-intake timepoint for alkaloids
  • Set and setting effects not experimentally controlled

Despite these limitations, this work provides the first human metabolomic framework for psychedelic research.

Why This Matters

Understanding the metabolic architecture of psychedelic states may:

  • Clarify mechanisms of therapeutic action
  • Identify biological markers of treatment response
  • Inform precision psychiatry approaches
  • Reveal systemic biomarkers beyond receptor pharmacology
